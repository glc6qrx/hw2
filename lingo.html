Common Lingo which climbers use

Slang Translation
Beta Information on the moves or sequence to complete a climb
Beta Break A way to complete a climb in an unintended way by bypassing holds, moves, etc.
Dyno Dynamic move where the climber jumps to another hold
Flash Complete a route in the first try
Day Flash Complete a route in the first try of the day
Double Flash* Complete a route on the second try
Triple Flash* Complete a route on the third try
Quadruple Flash* Complete a route on the fourth try
Ace Flash* Complete a route on the fifth try
Let me show you how it's done* I'm going to try this route, but almost certainly fall on the first move
It can't be done* It can't be done by me (Cliff). Is a causal effect (if A then B) of the above slang and almost always comes after it.
Pulling a Cliff* Climb a route, but ultimately fail on the very last move to complete the route. Term comes from a group of outdoor climbers who came extremely close but weren't able to climb this one route which just so happened to be a cliff.
Asteriks means the saying is not actual jargon and just inside jokes with my climbing friends.
